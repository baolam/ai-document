            Is the issue of adversarial examples related to the fact that NNs are purely extensional models - that is, they ignore structure and intensionality? What I mean here is that a NN, in the end, is some series of function composition of an input vector: F1(F2(F3( ... (x1, x2, x3, ... ))) .. ) where x1, x2, x3, ... is the input vector. But in such a model, WE CAN ALWAYS have several input vectors that will produce the same output/classification. Unless I missed something very obvious (which is very likely), I don't think there is something called "adversarial examples" (x is and adversarial examples wrt y, but in another NN y is an adversarial examples for x). The problem is in the model itself - it is purely extensional and works on numeric data that in end can result in producing the same output for many different patterns (3 + 5 = 4 * 2 = 1 + 6 + 1 = ...)

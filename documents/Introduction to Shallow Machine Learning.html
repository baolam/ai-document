      The Core Definitions

      Machine Learning or Artificial Intelligence?

      Think of it this way, take the words "Machine" and "Artificial" out of the comparison, it's basically "Learning" vs. "Intelligence". Learning is nothing but a necessary step on the way of producing Intelligence, an essential component of an "Intelligent" system.

      I have put some thoughts on Learning , Intelligence, Consciousness and other A.I related topics in my article "A.I Theory of Making a Horse from Two Hundred Rabbits".

      Features and Weights

      A feature as defined in Christopher Bishop's book (Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning) is an individual measurable property or characteristic of a phenomenon being observed.

      How does our vision sensory recognize that a Neanderthal is different from a Silverback Gorilla?

      Is it the flat-nosed face? Is it the ears, the skull shape, the mouth, the facial hair? How Gorilla's eyes are different from a Neanderthal? I don't see the eyes as a big differentiator, do you ?; gorillas display similar amounts of the visible sclera as Neanderthal.

      This would be my order of the "Features" by their "Weights" to decide that the right side of the photo is not a Silverback Gorilla:

      -Nose , very high weight
-Mouth, very high weight
-Ears, very high weight
-Skull shape, very high weight
-Mouth, very high weight
-Facial hair, high weight
-Eyes, low weight
-Forehead, low weight
-Chest, low weight

      You may have different "Weighting" of these "Features", that's ok as long as we both agree there is a Gorilla in the picture :)

      The building block of Machine Learning is the ability of a ML Algorithm to weigh the features of the data fed to that Algorithm in order to make the most accurate prediction. A change of the shape of a nose for example can make a big difference in our decision if the face we are looking at is a face of a dog, a monkey, a horse of a human, it's a High Weight Feature.

      Computing the Weights of these Features is a big part of what Machine Learning is

      Another example to gain more clarity on the concept of features weighting, assume we are trying to predict a Price of a House in Dubai, what are the main features to consider? You may think of "The Number of Bed Rooms", "The Floor Plan", "The Location", "The Developer", "The Handover Date", "Amenities" or maybe more, these are the "Features" of the House. Some "Features" are essential, and they profoundly impact the price of the House that we trying to predict, and this is what we call the "Weight" of the "Feature", and this what the Machine Learning algorithm is primarily built to learn from the data.

      Shallow or Deep Learning ?

      Now, who to hire If we want to collect real-estate data about Houses in a City to build a Price Predictor Machine Learning Model? A Machine Learning Engineer or a Real-Estate Agent?

      You are correct if your answer was a Real-Estate Agent. Picking the right features of the data (Number of Bed Rooms, Location, etc.) and then determining the relation between them (Amenities' weight is highly dependent on the location, maybe they're a lot of amenities around, but the area it's not covered by a taxi service for example) is a job of a domain expert in the field of "Shallow Learning".

      The features extraction in Shallow Machine Learning is a manual process that requires domain knowledge of the data that we are learning from

      In other words, "Shallow Learning" is a type of machine learning where we learn from data described by pre-defined features.

      While in "Deep Learning" the feature extraction is algorithmically computed without manual human intervention, recalling the Gorilla example, a Deep Learning algorithm will automatically recognize the weighted features of a Gorilla's face ( flat nose, facial hair, etc) if the Deep Learning algorithm was trained on thousands or maybe with a more advanced algorithm only hundreds of pictures of Gorilla's faces.

      In the shallow learning example, the real-estate knowledge of what data to collect about the house is required before we train the Machine Learning Model on this data, while in Deep Learning we can recognize any shape pictured in a photo without knowledge about the features of that shape.

      A Deep learning algorithm automatically learns these features along with their weights.


      Domains of Shallow Machine Learning

      Shallow ML algorithms vary depending on their domain of applications and the nature of predication that we are trying to make; they can be grouped as followed:

      • Regression, where we learn from numeric labeled data to predict a quantity of an input. Popular applications are in business statistics, to evaluate trends and make estimates or forecasts, pricing and promotions on sales of a product, etc.
      • Classification, where we learn from labeled data to predict a classification of an input. Popular applications of Classification ML are in different domains.For example, Weather forecasting applications for predicting rainfall and weather conditions, Medical diagnosis, and other fields
      • Clustering, where we learn from unknown/unlabeled data to predict a cluster of an input. A very familiar example of clustering applications is grouping customers with similar traits and likelihood to purchase a product.
      • Reinforcement, the algorithm interacts with a certain environment, thus changing its state, and receives rewards or penalties for its input. Its goal is to find patterns of actions, by trying them all and comparing the results, that yield the most reward points. Robotics and Games are two example domains of Reinforcement Learning.

      The Intuition of Shallow ML Algorithms

      I will use two examples of Shallow Algorithms: Linear Regression and K-Means Clustering.

      Linear Regression Algorithm

      With Linear Regression we try to predict a continuous value of a given data point by learning from labeled data in hand, let's apply that on the house pricing example:

      House1: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
House2: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
House3: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
House4: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
House5: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
House6: BedRooms, Location, Amenities --> Price
...
..
Housex:Bedrooms, Location, Amenities --> ?

      Our core objective is to calculate the weights of the house features:

      X1=Number of Bed Rooms
X2=Location
X3=Amenities
X4=Crime Rate




θ1=Weight of X1
θ2=Weight of X2
θ3=Weight of X3
θ4=Weight of X4






Our hyphosis funcation of the house price (Y):

Y = X1*θ1 + X2*θ2 + X3*θ3 + X4*θ4

      How can we predict the house price (Y) if we know the Number of Bed Rooms (X1), Location (X2), Amenities (X3) and Crime Rate (X4)?

      By Learning from the pricing data of other houses (The Training Data Set):

      When training the model, it fits the best array of weights {θ1,θ2,θ3,θ4} to predict the value of Y for a given array of X {X1,X2,X3,X4}, The model gets the best regression fit line by finding the best θ1,θ2,θ3 and θ4 values.

      By achieving the best-fit regression line, the model aims to predict Y value such that the error difference between predicted value and true value is minimum. So, we keep trying new weight values (starting with a random array of weights), to reach the best values that minimize the error between predicted Y value and true Y value.

      The error of each array of weights that we try can be mathematically calculated as the Root Mean Squared Error of the predication value of Y minus the actual Y value, this is called the linear regression Cost Function (Large Error Value means High Cost of the assumed array of weights)
      Cost (θ) = RMSE (Predicted_Y - Actual_Y)

      Now to update θ (Weight) values in order to reduce Cost function (minimizing RMSE value) and achieving the best fit line the model, we use a technique called Gradient Descent.

      The idea is to start with random θ values and then iteratively updating the values to reach the minimum cost/error (difference between actual and predicted values of "Y").

      Once the right weights of the features are calculated, the linear regression model is ready for testing and deployment..

      K-Means Clustering Algorithm

      K-Means clustering is one of the shallow unsupervised machine learning algorithms. It makes inferences from datasets without referring to known, or labelled, outcomes.

      A cluster refers to a collection of data points aggregated together because of certain similarities.

      The objective of K-Means machine learning is to group/cluster data with similar features together. K, refers to the number of clusters that the algorithm infers from the Data (you need to tell the algorithm how many clusters to use for the learning process)

      K-Means algorithm does that by positioning an imaginary location representing the center of each cluster the data.These locations are called "Centroids".

      The results of the K-means clustering algorithm are the Centroids of the K clusters, which can be used to label new data.

      The K-Means learning process:

      -Pick random locations of the Centroids of the K number of clusters

Centroids []= {C1,C2,C4,C5}

-Measure the distance between all data points and the centriods, and then assign each data point to it's nearest Centriod

Min Distance(X[],C[])

-Take the average value of all the distances between each Centroid and it's assigned data points

-Move the Centroids to new locations, the new location of a centroid is equal to the average distance value calculated above

-Keep iterating until the distance between a cluster centroid and it's dataset is mininum
